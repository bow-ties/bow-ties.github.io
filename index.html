Background

I am a STEM educator combining nuanced views of artistic and scientific practices to explore new pedagogical presentations of knowledge production.

  • Currently, I am a Technical Instructor in the Physics Instructional Resources Lab in the Physics Education Group at the MIT Department of Physics where I run lecture demos and labs as well as problem solving for TEAL classes in addition to managing the physics machine shops.
  • Currently my duties are:
    • Compiling the history of our department's educational efforts for our website
    • Researching our historical demonstration and lab apparatus to present as a talk for the AAPT Summer Conference.
    • Resurrecting an ultra-high vacuum pressure of light demo.
    • Developing Laboratory-Specific Chemical Hygiene Training as an EHS Representative.
  • In 2021, I graduated from MIT with an SB in Physics and a minor in Art, Culture, and Technology.
  • As a student, I managed a multi-purpose STEAM makerspace known as the Edgerton Center Student Project Lab (4-409).
  • In 2017, I was the valedictorian of Richland High School in suburban Johnstown, PA.
